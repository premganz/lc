<p class="Default"><strong><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">UNDERSTANDING SOME IMPORTANT CONCEPTS </span></strong></p>
<p class="Default"><strong><u><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">I. AMALGAMATION/MERGER &ndash; </span></u></strong></p>
<p class="Default"><strong><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">Accounting Standard 14 </span></strong><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">defines amalgamation as &ndash; </span></p>
<p class="Default"><em><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">&ldquo;Amalgamation means an amalgamation pursuant to the provisions of Companies Act, 1956 or any other statute which may be applicable to companies&rdquo;. </span></em></p>
<p class="Default"><strong><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">AS 14 provides for two types of amalgamation </span></strong><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">&ndash; &ldquo;Amalgamation in the nature&rdquo; of merger and &ldquo;Amalgamation in the nature of purchase&rdquo;.<strong>Amalgamation in the nature of merger </strong>is an amalgamation which satisfies all the following conditions. </span></p>
<p class="Default"><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">(i) All the assets and liabilities of the transferor company become, after amalgamation, the assets and liabilities of the transferee company. </span></p>
<p class="Default" style="page-break-before: always;"><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">&nbsp;(ii) Shareholders holding not less than 90% of the face value of the equity shares of the transferor company (other than the equity shares already held therein, immediately before the amalgamation, by the transferee company or its subsidiaries or their nominees) become equity shareholders of the transferee company by virtue of the amalgamation. </span></p>
<p class="Default"><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">(iii) The consideration for the amalgamation receivable by those equity shareholders of the transferor company who agree to become equity shareholders of the transferee company is discharged by the transferee company wholly by the issue of equity shares in the transferee company, except that cash may be paid in respect of any fractional shares. </span></p>
<p class="Default"><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">(iv) The business of the transferor company is intended to be carried on, after the amalgamation, by the transferee company. </span></p>
<p class="Default"><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">(v) No adjustment is intended to be made to the book values of the assets and liabilities of the transferor company when they are incorporated in the financial statements of the transferee company except to ensure uniformity of accounting policies. </span></p>
<p class="Default"><strong><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">Amalgamation in the nature of purchase </span></strong><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">is an amalgamation which does not satisfy any one or more of the conditions specified in sub-paragraph (e) above. </span></p>
<p class="Default"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">According to Income Tax Act amalgamation means: </span></p>
<p class="Default"><strong><em><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">"amalgamation</span></em></strong><em><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">", in relation to companies, means the merger of one or more companies with another company or the merger of two or more companies to form one company (the company or companies which so merge being referred to as the amalgamating company or companies and the company with which they merge or which is formed as a result of the merger, as the amalgamated company) in such a manner that &ndash; </span></em></p>
<p class="Default"><em><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">(i) all the property of the amalgamating company or companies immediately before the amalgamation becomes the property of the amalgamated company by virtue of the amalgamation ; </span></em></p>
<p class="Default"><em><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">(ii) all the liabilities of the amalgamating company or companies immediately before the amalgamation become the liabilities of the amalgamated company by virtue of the amalgamation; </span></em></p>
<p class="Default"><em><span style="font-size: 10.5pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">(iii) shareholders holding not less than 3three-fourths in value of the shares in the amalgamating company or companies (other than shares already held therein immediately before the amalgamation by, or by a nominee for, the amalgamated company or its subsidiary) become shareholders of the amalgamated company by virtue of the amalgamation. </span></em></p>
<p class="Default"><strong><u><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">II. DEMERGER </span></u></strong></p>
<p class="Default"><strong><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">Section 2(19AA) of the Income Tax Act, 1961 </span></strong><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">added by the Finance Act, 1999 provides that &ldquo;demerger&rdquo; in relation to companies, means the transfer, pursuant to a scheme of arrangement under sections 391 to 394 of the Companies Act, 1956 (1 of 1956), by a demerged company of its one or more undertakings to any resulting company in such a manner that&mdash; </span></p>
<p style="box-sizing: border-box;">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="Default" style="page-break-before: always;"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; font-family: 'Bookman Old Style',serif; mso-bidi-font-family: 'Bookman Old Style';">&nbsp;(<em>i</em>) all the property of the undertaking, being transferred by the demerged company, immediately before the demerger, becomes the property of the resulting company by virtue of the demerger; (<em>ii</em>) all the liabilities relatable to the undertaking, being transferred by the demerged company, immediately before the demerger, become the liabilities of the resulting company by virtue of the demerger; (<em>iii</em>) the property and the liabilities of the undertaking or undertakings being transferred by the demerged company are transferred at values appearing in its books of account immediately before the demerger; (<em>iv</em>) the resulting company issues, in consideration of the demerger, its shares to the shareholders of the demerged company on a proportionate basis; (<em>v</em>) the shareholders holding not less than three-fourths in value of the shares in the demerged company (other than shares already held therein immediately before the demerger, or by a nominee for, the resulting company or, its subsidiary) become share-holders of the resulting company or companies by virtue of the demerger, otherwise than as a result of the acquisition of the property or assets of the demerged company or any undertaking thereof by the resulting company; (<em>vi</em>) the transfer of the undertaking is on a going concern basis; (<em>vii</em>) the demerger is in accordance with the conditions, if any, notified under sub-section (5) of section 72A by the Central Government in this behalf. In cases of demergers where only one of the many undertakings or part of an undertaking is transferred as an exercise in corporate restructuring, the transferor company would continue to exist to carry on its other businesses. However in case where all the undertakings of a business are transferred to different transferee companies, there is no need for the transferor company to exist and therefore it can be dissolved without winding up.&nbsp;</span></p>
